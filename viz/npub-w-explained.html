Understanding "W" — The Window Size

W is simply: how many consecutive characters are we looking at?

The Sliding Window

Imagine a magnifying glass that slides across the NPUB. W = how wide the lens is.
Same NPUB, same position, different window sizes:
npub1q p z r y q p z r y q p z r y ...
W=3: look at 3 chars at a time
q p z r y q p z r y q p z r y
W=5: look at 5 chars at a time
q p z r y q p z r y q p z r y
W=8: look at 8 chars at a time
q p z r y q p z r y q p z r y
W=16: look at 16 chars at a time
q p z r y q p z r y q p z r y
Why does W matter?

A short window (W=3) asks: "Are these 3 characters unusually repetitive?"
A long window (W=16) asks: "Are these 16 characters unusually repetitive?"

A pattern like "qqq" is surprising at W=3 but meaningless at W=16.
A pattern like "qpqpqpqpqpqpqpqp" is EXTREMELY surprising at W=16 but normal at W=3.

We scan all W values (3,4,5,...,20) to catch outliers at every scale.
